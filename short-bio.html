A Full Stack Developer is someone who works with the Back End — or server side — of the application as well as the Front End, or client side. Full Stack Developers have to have some skills in a wide variety of coding niches, from databases to graphic design and UI/UX management in order to do their job well. We are something of a swing, ready to assist wherever needed in the process.

For instance, a small independent game group made up of only a few people creating a small simulator could have one Back End Developer and one Front End Developer and that might be all needed for the development. If they’re even smaller, they might have one Full Stack Developer who handles the entire application. On the other hand, a large web application with strong potential for scaling will require many hands on deck — Back End, Front End, and Full Stack Developers.

Developer Skills

A full-stack developer as aforementioned is not a multifaceted role. To excel at this role one requisite, not just a formal degree instead, extensive knowledge of a variety of skills. Diversity is the key to excellence and success in the profession of a full-stack developer.

For learning the front-end development, one would be requiring to learn HTML, CSS which determine the presentation of a page and JavaScript programming language that determines the function. For back end, the essentials are Node.js, SQL, MongoDB etc. Thus a full-stack web developer would be furnished. Pair the above with sound knowledge of Data Structures & Algorithms and one is sure to develop efficient code that brings space-time complexity down to its minimum possible range.
